The math of hidden costs

A typical app might charge a small $1.99 fee to send $200. This looks like a good deal at first. But that same app often adds a markup of about 2.7% to the exchange rate. On a $200 transfer, that hidden cost adds up to about $5.40. Your total cost to send that money is $7.39, not just the $1.99 fee you saw first.

Compare this to a service that uses the mid-market exchange rate with no markup. If you send $200 at the real rate, your family could get about 11,148 PHP. With a typical hidden markup, they might only get 10,847 PHP. That is 301 pesos less for your loved ones from a single transfer.

Over time, these small gaps grow into large sums of money. If you send money every month, you could lose thousands of pesos each year. That money could pay for school, food, or bills for your family in the Philippines. Choosing the right rate is just as vital as finding a low fee.

What is the mid-market rate

The mid-market rate is the real rate that banks use to trade money with each other. It is the midpoint between the buy and sell prices of a currency. This is the fairest rate possible because it has zero markup added to it. You can find this rate on sites like Google or Reuters to check if an app is giving you a fair deal.

Many apps will not show you this rate. They will show you their own rate instead. If their rate is lower than the one you see on Google, they are charging a markup. This markup is a fee that you do not see in the fee list. It is an extra cost that takes money away from your family.
