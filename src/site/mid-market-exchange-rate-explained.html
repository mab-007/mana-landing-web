What Is a Mid-Market Exchange Rate and Why It Matters for Remittances

Sending money home is one of the most important things you do as an Overseas Filipino Worker. Every peso counts when you are supporting family back home. Yet hidden exchange rate markups quietly take money away from your hard-earned savings with every transfer. Understanding the mid-market exchange rate is the key to protecting your income and ensuring every dollar you send reaches the people who depend on it.

The mid-market exchange rate explained simply is the midpoint between the price a bank buys currency and the price it sells it. It is the real, fair rate that banks use when trading money with each other on the global market. Many services advertise zero fees but hide a markup by giving you a rate 2% to 5% worse than the mid-market rate. This hidden cost can drain hundreds of dollars from your hard-earned savings every year. By ensuring you get the real mid-market rate, you keep more of your money working for your family in the Philippines.

Armed with the right knowledge, you can spot hidden fees disguised as exchange rates and make smarter choices about how you send money abroad. The difference between a fair rate and a marked-up one could mean an extra grocery trip for your family, a paid utility bill, or savings that grow over time.

Mid-Market Exchange Rate Explained: What Is the Mid-Market Exchange Rate?

When you send money home, you might see a rate that looks different from what you find on Google. This is because most apps show you a rate with a markup. To understand your true costs, you must first know about the mid-market exchange rate. This rate, often called the middle rate or interbank rate, is the midpoint between the buy and sell prices of two currencies. It is the fairest rate you can get because it has no extra fees added to it.

How the rate is set

In global markets, buyers and sellers trade money all day. The price buyers want to pay is the bid price. The price sellers want to get is the ask price. The mid-market rate is the average of these two prices. Banks and big trade firms use this formula to trade between themselves: (bid + ask) / 2. This rate changes every second based on how many people want to buy or sell a currency at that time.

Supply and demand in the market

Global supply and demand drive these price changes. If many people want to buy Philippine pesos, the price goes up. If more people want to sell them, the price goes down. Most people who send money home through apps do not know how this works. In fact, reports show that 45 percent of remitters do not know what the mid-market rate is. This can lead to paying more for your transfers without even knowing it.

Spreads for different currencies

Some pairs of money are easy to trade, like the U.S. dollar and the Euro. These have a tight gap between the buy and sell prices. Other pairs, like the dollar and the peso, can have wider gaps. This makes the mid-point even more vital for near-zero fee remittance. Knowing this rate helps you see if a provider is giving you a fair deal or hiding costs in the exchange rate.

The Hidden Cost of Moving Money Across Borders

When you send money home to the Philippines, the fee you see is rarely the full price. Most banks and apps use a secret charge called an exchange rate markup to make more profit. This makes it hard for Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) to know how much money will reach their family. These costs add up fast and take money away from the people who need it most.

You can see how this works by looking at the real mid-market rates used by fair providers.

Many companies use smart ads to hide these gaps. About 72% of people who send money believe they know how fees work. But only 18% truly understand how markups change their transfers. Watch Out for Zero Fee Claims

Be careful when an app says it has 0% fees or no commission. These firms often give you a much worse exchange rate to make up for the fee. This trap can cost you thousands of pesos over a year. Always check the rate on a search site or a rate tracker before you send money. If the rate in your app is lower than the real rate, you are paying a hidden tax on your transfer.

How to Check If You Are Getting the Real Exchange Rate

Now that you understand the mid-market exchange rate and how hidden markups work, here is how to protect your money. Follow these simple steps before every transfer to make sure your family gets the most out of every dollar you send.

  1. Look up the current mid-market rate. Search online for the current exchange rate between USD and PHP. Google, XE.com, and OANDA all show the real mid-market rate. Bookmark one of these sites so you can check before every transfer. The rate changes throughout the day, so checking at the time you send gives you the most accurate comparison.
  2. Compare with your provider's rate. Most remittance apps show the rate they will use before you confirm the transfer. Compare this rate with the mid-market rate you found online. If the provider's rate is lower, you are paying a hidden markup. The difference might seem small, but it adds up over time.
  3. Watch for "0% fee" marketing. When a provider advertises zero fees or no commission, they are almost always hiding costs in the exchange rate. No business works for free. If you do not see a fee, look for a worse rate instead. The total cost of your transfer includes both the fee and the rate markup, so always look at the full picture.
  4. Calculate total cost in pesos received, not fees paid. The only number that truly matters is how many pesos your family receives. A low-fee service with a bad exchange rate can leave your family with less money than a service that charges a modest fee but offers a fair rate. Always compare the final amount in pesos, not the advertised fee in dollars.

Frequently Asked Questions

When you send money home, getting the real exchange rate matters more than the advertised fee. Here are answers to common questions about the mid-market exchange rate and how it affects your remittances.

What is the mid-market exchange rate?

The mid-market exchange rate, also called the interbank rate or middle rate, is the midpoint between the buying price and selling price of two currencies. It is calculated as (bid + ask) / 2 and represents the fairest possible exchange rate without any profit margins or fees added.

Is the mid-market exchange rate the best rate available?

Yes, the mid-market rate is the most genuine and fairest exchange rate because it reflects the true market value without hidden markups. However, most banks and money transfer services do not offer this rate to retail customers. They add a margin or spread on top to generate profit, which is why comparing rates is important.

Why do banks not use the mid-market rate for customers?

Banks and money transfer services use the mid-market rate when trading between themselves, but they rarely pass it on to individual customers. Instead, they mark up the rate to cover operating costs and generate profit. This markup is often hidden in the exchange rate rather than shown as an upfront fee, making it difficult for customers to see the true cost.

How much money do hidden FX markups cost OFW families?

Hidden foreign exchange markups cost Filipino families an estimated $500 million to $1 billion every year. A typical OFW sending $1,000 monthly can lose $14 to $130 per transfer to hidden costs. Over a year of regular remittances, these unnecessary fees add up to $168 to $1,560 that could have gone to the family instead.

How can I get the mid-market exchange rate for my remittances?

Most traditional banks and remittance services do not offer the mid-market rate to customers. However, some financial technology companies like Mana provide the real mid-market exchange rate with no hidden markup and a near-zero flat fee. What is the difference between the mid-market rate and the rate banks offer?

The mid-market rate is the true market rate without any markup. Banks and remittance services add a margin on top of this rate, typically 2% to 5%, to generate profit. This means that if the true mid-market rate is 1 USD = 55 PHP, a bank might offer you 1 USD = 53 PHP, keeping the difference of 2 PHP per dollar as profit.
