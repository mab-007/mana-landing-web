The High Cost of Sending Money Home

Sending money across borders often comes with high costs that are hard to see. According to data from The World Bank Remittance Prices Worldwide, Filipinos lose between $500 million and $1 billion every year to hidden rate markups hidden in exchange rate spreads. Key takeaway: A study by the World Bank found that 72 percent of people sending money claim to understand these fees, but only 18 percent truly know how much they are paying. Barriers in Standard US Banking

Opening an account at a big bank in the US is often very hard for Filipinos living abroad. Most traditional banks require:

  • A Social Security Number (SSN) or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)
  • A physical US residential address for mailing
  • An in-person branch visit to verify identification documents
  • A US-based phone number for account verification
