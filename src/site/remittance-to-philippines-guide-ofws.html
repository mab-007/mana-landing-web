Understanding Exchange Rates for Remittance to Philippines

Exchange rates determine how many pesos your family receives for every dollar you send. The mid-market rate is the real rate with no markup added. Sending $500 at the mid-market rate of 56.00 PHP gives your family 28,000 PHP. A 2% markup drops that to 27,440 PHP, and a 4% markup brings it to 26,880 PHP. Even a small markup costs your family hundreds of pesos each transfer.

When you send money home, the exchange rate is often more important than the transfer fee. Most people look at the upfront cost to send money. However, a large part of what you pay is hidden in the rate. Learning how rates work helps you make sure your family gets the most pesos possible.

What is the mid-market rate?

The mid-market rate is the real exchange rate. It is the center point between the buy and sell prices on the global market. This is the rate you see when you check Google Finance. It is the fairest rate because it has no extra fees added to it.

Many banks and transfer services do not use this rate. Instead, they give you a retail rate. This rate is the mid-market rate plus a small markup. Even a small markup can take a lot of money away from your family over time.

How hidden markups affect your transfer

Hidden costs are a big problem in remittance to Philippines. Research from the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas shows that 72% of people think they understand fees, but only 18% actually do. About 45% of users do not know what a mid-market rate is. This lets services hide costs in the exchange rate.

Banks often add a 3% to 5% markup on top of their wire fees. A service might claim to have low fees, but they make up for it with a poor rate. This is why you should always compare the offered rate to the real rate before sending your money.

The math of a $500 transfer

Let us look at how rates change the amount of pesos your family gets. If you send $500 when the real rate is 56.00 PHP, your family should get 28,000 PHP. If a service adds a 2% markup, the rate drops to 54.88 PHP. Your family now gets 27,440 PHP. You just lost 560 PHP.

At a 4% markup, the rate becomes 53.76 PHP. Your family gets only 26,880 PHP. That is 1,120 PHP less than the real value. By getting the best PHP exchange rate, you keep that money for your loved ones. Always check the total pesos delivered, not just the fee.
