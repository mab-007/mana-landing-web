ITIN vs SSN: What Filipino Immigrants Need to Know

Get clear answers on ITIN vs SSN for Filipino immigrants. Learn key differences, application steps, and how to build US financial history with either number.

Moving to the United States means learning a new system of paperwork, identification numbers, and financial rules. For Filipino immigrants, one of the first and most important questions is the difference between an ITIN and an SSN. Understanding which number you need shapes your ability to file taxes, open a bank account, and build a financial future in America.

ITIN vs SSN is a common question for immigrants who need to interact with the US tax and employment systems. An Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) is a tax processing number issued by the IRS to people who must file taxes but cannot get a Social Security Number. In contrast, a Social Security Number (SSN) is issued to US citizens and authorized residents to track earnings and manage government benefits. According to the IRS, the ITIN is only for federal tax reporting and does not grant legal work authorization or change immigration status. While both numbers have nine digits and look similar, they serve fundamentally different roles for Filipinos living and working in America.

This guide breaks down each option so you can choose the right path for your taxes, banking, and long-term financial goals. We start with the basic definitions and work through the practical decisions you will face.

What Are ITIN and SSN?

Two of the most common identification numbers in the US are the Social Security Number (SSN) and the Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). They look alike, but they serve very different purposes for Filipino immigrants.

What is an ITIN?

The Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) is a tax processing number the IRS issues to people who must file taxes but cannot get an SSN. This group includes nonresident aliens, their spouses, and their dependents. If you are a Filipino freelancer working remotely for US clients without a work visa, you likely need an ITIN.

An ITIN has nine digits and always starts with the number 9, following the 9XX-XX-XXXX format. The IRS uses it to track tax returns and payments. However, having an ITIN does not grant legal work authorization or change your immigration status. It is purely a tool for tax compliance.

What is an SSN?

The Social Security Number (SSN) is also a nine-digit number, but it is issued by the Social Security Administration (SSA) rather than the IRS. Originally created to track earnings for retirement benefits, the SSN has become the primary identification number for almost every aspect of life in the US.

Most Filipino nurses, teachers, and other professionals on work visas receive an SSN when they start their jobs. This number is essential for building a financial life in the US. You need it to apply for credit cards, rent an apartment, or get a driver's license in many states. OFWs and Filipino professionals moving to the US should prioritize getting an SSN if they qualify.

Key differences between the issuing agencies

The IRS handles ITINs exclusively for tax filing purposes. The SSA issues SSN cards to citizens and individuals with work authorization. If you qualify for an SSN, you should always apply for that first rather than an ITIN.

Primary purposes

The ITIN exists so people who cannot get an SSN can still meet their tax obligations. The IRS issues it solely for tax administration. In contrast, the SSA created the SSN to track worker earnings and social security benefits. Most Filipino workers will need one or both of these numbers to stay compliant with US law. While an SSN lasts a lifetime, an ITIN expires if not used on a tax return for three consecutive years.

Work authorization and immigration status

This is the most important distinction. An ITIN does not grant the right to work in the US or change your immigration status. The IRS issues ITINs only for tax administration. For many Filipino freelancers earning US income from abroad, an ITIN is simply a tool to report and pay taxes on that income. Even if a bank accepts an ITIN to open an account, it does not prove legal residency. Employers and financial institutions overwhelmingly prefer an SSN because it connects to work records and credit history.

Transitioning from ITIN to SSN

If your immigration status changes and you become eligible for an SSN, you must stop using your ITIN. Using both numbers simultaneously violates IRS rules and can create tax complications. You need to notify the IRS to link your old tax records to your new SSN so you receive full credit for all taxes previously paid.

Who Needs Which Number? A Guide for Filipino Immigrants

Choosing between an ITIN and an SSN depends on your visa status and how you earn income. Most Filipinos come to the US to support their families back home. And getting the right tax identification number is a key step toward building a stable financial life and accessing financial tools designed for OFWs.

Work visa holders

If you arrive in the US with a work visa, you generally need a Social Security Number. This includes Filipino nurses on H-1B visas, teachers on J-1 exchange visas, and other professionals. Your SSN tracks your earnings and contributions to US benefits programs. Apply for your SSN as soon as you arrive. Hospitals, clinics, and schools will require it before your first paycheck.

Freelancers and remote workers

Many Filipinos work remotely for US companies while living in the Philippines. If you are a freelancer based in the Philippines earning US income, you likely need an ITIN. This number allows you to file US taxes on income from American clients. The ITIN is strictly for tax filing and does not authorize you to work inside the US. It is a compliance tool that helps you build a legitimate US income record while working from abroad.

Family members and students

If your visa does not allow immediate work, such as a dependent spouse or someone awaiting a green card, you may qualify for an ITIN. The IRS issues ITINs to resident and nonresident aliens, spouses, and dependents who have a US tax filing requirement but no SSN. International students on F-1 visas can get an SSN only if they have on-campus employment. Undocumented workers earning US income should use an ITIN to file taxes, which demonstrates compliance and may support future legal status applications. Remember, an ITIN does not change your immigration status. Once you become eligible for an SSN, you must switch.

Can You Work in the US With an ITIN?

Many people believe that having an ITIN gives them the right to work in the United States. This is a common misunderstanding that can lead to serious legal issues. While an ITIN allows you to file taxes, it does not grant work authorization or change your immigration status. To hold a job legally in the US, you generally need a Social Security Number backed by valid work authorization.

ITIN is for taxes only, not employment

The IRS issues ITINs for one purpose: to enable tax filing. It is not a work permit, a green card, or any form of immigration status. According to the IRS, an ITIN is for federal tax administration only and does not authorize employment. You must comply with immigration laws to work legally in the US.

You may still need an ITIN if you are a nonresident earning income from US sources, such as rental property or business income. Even if you live abroad, the IRS expects you to report this income, and an ITIN makes that possible.

SSN and work authorization

To work for a US employer, you must have proper work authorization. An SSN is the tool that tracks your earnings and contributions to Social Security and Medicare. Employers use Form I-9 to verify work eligibility, and an ITIN cannot satisfy this requirement. If you are a Filipino worker seeking US employment, you must first obtain work authorization through USCIS. Only then can you get an SSN and legally start working.

Filing taxes on your US income

If you earn US income as a freelancer or remote worker, you can use an ITIN to file your taxes. Paying taxes on your US income builds a compliance record that may support future visa or green card applications. It is better to report and pay what you owe than to risk penalties for non-filing.

How to Apply for an ITIN: Step by Step

Getting an ITIN is essential if you live in the US or earn US income but cannot obtain an SSN. The Mana waitlist can help you prepare for your financial future, but securing your tax identification number is the first step toward compliance. Your ITIN will be a nine-digit number starting with 9, following the same format as an SSN (9XX-XX-XXXX).

  1. Complete Form W-7. This is the official IRS application for an ITIN. You must explain why you need the number and provide your name and mailing address.
  2. Gather original identification documents. You need to prove your identity and foreign status. A valid passport is the most widely accepted document.
  3. Prepare your federal tax return. In most cases, you must attach a completed tax return to your W-7 to demonstrate a legitimate tax filing purpose.
  4. Submit your application package. Mail everything to the IRS, visit a Taxpayer Assistance Center in person, or use a Certifying Acceptance Agent to avoid mailing your original passport.
  5. Wait for processing. The IRS typically takes 7 to 11 weeks to process ITIN applications. Your ITIN arrives by mail once approved.

Using a Certifying Acceptance Agent

A Certifying Acceptance Agent is an IRS-authorized professional who can verify your identity documents so you do not have to mail your original passport to the IRS. This is especially helpful for Filipino families who want to keep their documents safe. These agents are available in most major US cities with significant Filipino communities.

Building US Financial History Without an SSN

You can start building a US financial footprint even without a Social Security Number. Many new arrivals believe they must wait for an SSN to open a bank account, but an ITIN can serve that purpose at most major banks. This is one of the most practical advantages of understanding the ITIN vs SSN distinction for new arrivals. It allows you to save money and establish credit while you wait for permanent residency or work authorization.

Using an ITIN for banking and credit

An ITIN is primarily a tax number, but it also enables you to open a US bank account and begin building a credit history. Credit bureaus can track your payment record using your ITIN, which helps demonstrate creditworthiness even without an SSN. Paying bills on time with an ITIN on file can qualify you for a car loan or a secured credit card later. It is a practical way to establish yourself financially in a new country.

A bank account also simplifies everyday tasks like paying rent, setting up utilities, and receiving direct deposits from US clients. This transition from a cash-based to a digital financial life protects your money and gives you more control over your monthly finances.

Moving from ITIN to SSN

An ITIN serves you well in the short term, but it does not authorize employment in the US. Once you obtain work authorization and receive an SSN, you must notify the IRS to link your tax records. This ensures your full earnings history and tax payments transfer to your new SSN, preserving access to tax credits, refunds, and future Social Security benefits.

Transitioning From ITIN to SSN

Many Filipinos begin their US journey filing taxes with an ITIN. When your immigration status changes and you become eligible for a Social Security Number, you must take specific steps to ensure a smooth transition. This shift from ITIN to SSN marks a significant milestone in your financial life.

Notify the IRS of your new SSN

Once you receive an SSN, you must stop using your ITIN for tax purposes immediately. Using both numbers simultaneously is against IRS rules and can delay your tax returns or create errors that are difficult to correct later. Transitioning to your SSN ensures your US earnings are properly tracked for future Social Security and Medicare benefits.

Combine your tax records

You want the IRS to transfer all your prior tax data from your ITIN to your new SSN. This is especially important for OFWs who worked for years using an ITIN before gaining legal status. Without this linkage, you might lose credit for years of tax payments, potentially reducing future refunds or Social Security benefits.

To combine your records, send a signed letter to the IRS office where you file your taxes. Include your full name, current address, old ITIN, new SSN, and a copy of your Social Security card. This tells the IRS that all prior tax filings belong to the same person.

Update your financial accounts

After notifying the IRS, update your records with your employer so they use your SSN for payroll and tax forms. You should also update your bank accounts, credit cards, and any other financial accounts. For OFWs and freelancers, having an SSN on file helps build a stronger credit profile. Making it easier to qualify for car loans, mortgages, and better credit card terms in the future.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I open a bank account with an ITIN?

Yes, many US banks and credit unions allow you to open an account using your ITIN instead of an SSN. Banks use the ITIN to verify your identity and meet federal Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements. Policies vary by institution, so call ahead to confirm they accept ITINs before visiting a branch. Mana goes further by letting you open a US dollar account with just a Philippine address and valid ID, no SSN or ITIN required to get started.

Can I apply for a credit card with an ITIN?

Yes, several major credit card issuers accept ITINs for credit card applications. You may need to start with a secured credit card to build your credit history. Paying your balance on time each month helps establish a positive credit profile linked to your ITIN. Over time, this can qualify you for unsecured cards with better terms and rewards.

Does an ITIN give me the right to work in the US?

No. An ITIN is a tax processing number issued by the IRS for federal tax reporting only. According to the IRS, having an ITIN does not change your immigration status or authorize employment. You still need a valid work visa or employment authorization document to work legally in the US.

When does an ITIN expire?

Your ITIN expires if you do not use it on a federal tax return at least once every three years. For example, if you last used your ITIN on your 2021 tax return and did not file in 2022. 2023, or 2024, it expires at the end of 2024. The IRS requires renewal if you need to file after an expiration. Renewal processing can take several weeks, so plan ahead.

What should I do if I get an SSN after having an ITIN?

Stop using your ITIN immediately and notify the IRS so they can link your previous tax records to your new SSN. According to the IRS, failing to link these records means you may not receive proper credit for taxes you already paid. Send a signed letter to the IRS with your full name, old ITIN, new SSN. And a copy of your Social Security card to ensure your tax history transfers correctly.
